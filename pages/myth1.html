Understanding HIV/AIDS: Myths vs. Facts

Empowering through truth. Overcoming stigma together.

If you or someone you know needs support, please reach out to HIV Support Resources.

MYTHS

1. You can get HIV from casual contact, like hugging, kissing, or sharing utensils.

2. Mosquitoes can transmit HIV.

3. You can tell if someone has HIV by looking at them.

4. Only certain groups of people get HIV.

5. Once you have HIV, you will eventually develop AIDS and die.

FACTS

1. HIV is primarily spread through specific bodily fluids. Casual contact does not transmit the virus.

2. Mosquitoes cannot transmit HIV.

3. There are no visible signs that can definitively identify someone with HIV.

4. HIV can affect anyone, regardless of sexual orientation, gender, or ethnicity.

5. With proper medical care, people with HIV can live long, healthy lives.
